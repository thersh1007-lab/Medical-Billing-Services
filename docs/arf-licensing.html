What is an ARF license in California?

ARFs are licensed through California's Community Care Licensing (CCLD/CDSS) and provide 24-hour, non-medical care and supervision for adults who need support with daily living and safety.

Population served

Generally adults 18-59 (with specific allowances for certain 60+ situations), often with mental, physical, or developmental disabilities.

Type of care

24-hour non-medical care and supervision. Focus is on safety, assistance, and day-to-day support.

Oversight

Licensed and monitored under California's Community Care Licensing (CDSS/CCLD) system.

ARF (Adult Residential Facility) Adult-focused residential care model (often ages 18-59) with 24-hour non-medical supervision and support.
RCFE (Residential Care Facility for the Elderly) Elder-focused residential care model (typically serving seniors). If you're primarily serving older adults, RCFE may be the right track.

ARF Licensing FAQs

Short answers to the most common questions.

What does ARF stand for?

ARF stands for Adult Residential Facility, a licensed facility providing 24-hour non-medical care and supervision for adults.

Is ARF the same as RCFE?

No. ARF typically focuses on adults (often 18-59), while RCFE focuses on elder care. If you're unsure, book a consult and we'll confirm the right facility type.

Is this legal advice?

No. We provide operational and administrative guidance to help you prepare clean materials and avoid preventable delays. For legal questions, we'll point you to the appropriate professional.
